<p>The Straits Times School Pocket Money Fund (STSPMF) partners with Ministry
of Education (MOE) in disbursing school pocket money to students from low-income
families.</p>
<p>Students who&nbsp;<strong><u>meet the criteria</u></strong>&nbsp;will
be able to apply for the fund through the school.</p>
<p>To apply, you&nbsp;may submit the&nbsp;<strong><a href="/files/Annex_A_B___2026_STSPMF_Application_Form___income_dec_form.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>softcopy application</u></a></strong>&nbsp;along
with supporting documents via our school's email&nbsp;(<a href="mailto:aes@moe.edu.sg" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">aes@moe.edu.sg</a>),
subject title “<em>STSPMF 2026 Cycle 2</em>” or hardcopy application to
the general office.</p>
<p>Application deadline is on&nbsp;<strong><u>13 Feb 2026</u></strong>.</p>
<p><strong><em>***Note: <u>Students under MOE/SPED FAS or Com Care Assistance</u> will only need to submit Annex A (Application form) with the following document:</em></strong>
</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>MOE/SPED FAS or Com Care Assistance letter indicating period of assistance (Applicant/Student must be under assistance when applying for STSPMF.)</strong>
</p>
<p><strong><u>Eligibility Criteria</u></strong>
</p>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Student is a Singapore Citizen (SC) or Singapore Permanent Resident (SPR)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Family has a gross per capita income (PCI) of <strong>$1000</strong>/month
or less (to be reviewed from time-to-time)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Student is receiving full-time formal education in a mainstream secondary</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Is not concurrently receiving school pocket money fund from disbursing
agencies</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Is not concurrently receiving other similar monthly school pocket money
schemes <em>(Note that applicants under the MOE/SPED Financial Assistance Scheme are eligible</em>)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Has not received the maximum 24 months of STSPMF assistance during their
entire primary to secondary schooling years</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p><strong><u>Information on STSPMF Applications through schools</u></strong>
<br>The Straits Times School Pocket Money Fund provides pocket money for children
from low-income families through disbursing schools on a monthly basis.</p>
<p>1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Beneficiaries enrolled in secondary education
will receive $100/month</p>
<p><strong>*** STSPMF beneficiaries are provided with a maximum of 24 months of assistance during their entire primary to secondary schooling years</strong>.</p>
