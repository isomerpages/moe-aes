A collaboration between a few IP Departments in the school, we want students to understand and appreciate that each subject does not work in silo. Science, Mathematics, Humanities are all interrelated and are infused in our daily lives. As such, we do not want students to restrict themselves within a border- the classroom. Instead, we hope students can see Learning beyond borders and bask in the wonders of the earth.

  

Through the planned activities, we hope that our students can experience the joy of learning beyond their classrooms.

  

Our Theme was “Learning Beyond Borders”.

  

There are several special programmes in the AES Learning Festival.

  
### A Thought-Provoking Level Assembly Programme

  
Students get to Engage in MAGICAL demonstrations and use the SLIDO platform to formulate some explanations of what has happened in the demonstrations. The teachers co-organised this meaningful level assembly programme with the Secondary 4 Young Scientists.

### A Mass Amazing Race

The Science, Mathematics and Humanities Departments collaborated to design an Amazing Race for Secondary 1 students. In this race, students use a mobile app to roam around the school, answer Humanities, Math and Science questions, and perform hands-on experiments. The questions were designed based on the Secondary 1 curriculum. Through the Science experiments, Humanities tasks and the Mathematical tasks, students collaborated in groups, learnt to think critically and build up their problem-solving skills.
