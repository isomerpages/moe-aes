<p>AI literacy is rapidly becoming a necessary skillset for our workforce
to remain competitive and for Singapore to stay relevant on the world stage.
At the <a href="https://www.moe.gov.sg/news/press-releases/20260303-we-learn-for-life-together-building-a-we-first-society-through-education" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>MOE Committee of Supply (COS) debate 2026</u></a>,
MOE announced four pillars to strengthen students’ AI literacy:&nbsp;</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p>Learn about AI</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Learn to use AI</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Learn with AI&nbsp;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Learn beyond AI</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>At AES, we deliberately seek opportunities to infuse AI literacy education
into our curriculum. Recognising the developmental needs of different age
groups, we have designed an <strong>AI literacy curriculum tailored to different levels</strong>.
These learning objectives are taught either explicitly through stand-alone
lessons, or infused within existing CCE/MAP lessons or other curriculum
areas.</p>

<p>A large proportion of the AI tools that AES students are exposed to reside
in the Singapore Student Learning Space (see: <a href="https://www.learning.moe.edu.sg/ai-in-sls/about-ai-in-sls/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>About AI in SLS</u></a>).
The <strong>AI-enabled features in SLS </strong>have additional guardrails
to ensure a safe learning experience for students.</p>
<p>Concurrently, teachers regularly participate in <strong>professional development (PD) sessions </strong>to
discuss the impact of AI on teaching and learning.</p>
